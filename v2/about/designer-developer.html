Working as a Designer and Developer

During the first 8 years of my career, I switched between web design, front-end development, and UI design roles. While my focus was mostly on the creative side, I also had to learn how to build products, which pushed me to develop a systematic way of thinking to complement my creativity.

A unique learning experience

This dual role gave me a lot of experiences and challenges as the industry evolved. I dealt with everything from table-based layouts and the introduction of CSS to the frustrations of IE6, as well as responsive design, MySQL, JS, SEO, and mobile-first design. My design skills also broadened during this time to include a wide range of product and visual design skills, as I was the lead designer within the organization.

How it informs my role today

As the complexity of tech stacks began to surpass my grasp around 2014, I made a conscious shift towards concentrating on Product design roles. Despite this transition, the experiences from my earlier career still enable me to bridge the gap between design teams, product managers, and engineers, fostering effective collaboration and understanding.

While rare I also have been known to step in and help with html, css tasks for projects if engineering resources are stretched.
