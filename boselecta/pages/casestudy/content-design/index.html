Content Design :
Turning Proactive Iteration into Strategic Alignment

A post-release design initiative that began as self-driven improvement, and evolved into a key reference for a future project.

Outcome

By proactively anticipating future needs, I was able to align my independently developed concept designs with the goals of the Product Management team—at a time when they were short on capacity during the discovery phase. Although this approach wasn’t part of a formal process, my initiative and attention to detail allowed us to bring a wide range of ideas to the table that otherwise might have been missed. Usability testing helped refine these concepts, and while some features were filtered out, the breadth of exploration led to strong insights and validated several valuable directions.

Context

As the lead designer on the au TsukasaPo app, I’ve seen its core purpose evolve—from primarily offering diagnostic tools to becoming a content-driven experience tailored to its daily active users.

A recent joint initiative between KDDI and Asurion sparked a renewal of the app’s core concept. The goal: to deliver more engaging daily digital content through a refreshed, contemporary UI—broadening its appeal to younger users while continuing to support its core audience of older, mid-to-low literacy users..

Background

A year earlier, I had led the design of a content feature that fell short due to MVP constraints and ongoing requirement creep. Motivated by both proactivity and some frustration, I continued working post-release—developing improved concepts and exploring ways to drive more UX-led changes in future phases. Although those follow-up phases never materialized, the desktop research and design work I had done gained traction with the product management team. Over time, these early concepts became influential and were ultimately adopted during the discovery phase.
