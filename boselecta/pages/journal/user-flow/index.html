Automated Userflow and Screen Repository for a sprint project

It's easy to skip mapping user flows, but when teams have a shared visual guide, decisions get sharper, and the product comes together more smoothly.

The Challenge

When I joined my current company, I quickly recognized the value of the app’s 200+ screen user flow in helping me get up to speed. However, each sprint cycle required me to manually update the flow, which became a time-consuming and repetitive task. Realizing an opportunity to improve this, I applied my systemized approach to automate the process using auto-layout and a screen repository.

"Userflows aren't glamorous, but many people rely on a userflow for a variety of tasks. When they work well, it's not seen, but when they fail, it's noticed."

Refining the Workflow

By streamlining the user flow updates, I automated the process of syncing changes across multiple screens, significantly reducing the time spent on manual revisions. I created a central screen repository, leveraging Figma’s library functionality to ensure consistency in variables, text, and detailing across the project. With the addition of auto-layout, the user flow and sections could evolve independently of UI changes, allowing for seamless updates when screens were adjusted. This systemized approach also kept the team aligned and minimized errors.

What This Unlocked

This automation not only freed up valuable time for deeper design iterations but also helped maintain consistency across the project. With a central repository and Figma libraries in place, updates became more efficient, and cross-team collaboration was improved. This process allowed the design to stay aligned with the development, ensuring that the most current version was always available, and helping us focus on delivering high-quality designs and a seamless user experience."

The Invisible Work

Often, the most valuable work goes unnoticed until it's missing. By streamlining processes, automating repetitive tasks, and ensuring consistency across design and development, I’ve freed up my team to focus on what truly matters. The time saved by refining workflows isn’t always visible, but the impact is clear—the smoother and more efficient our processes, the more space we have for creativity and problem-solving. The invisible work, like managing variables and maintaining consistency, is what makes everything else possible.
