Working as a Designer and Developer

From 2006 to 2016 I worked as both a designer and front end developer for a bookings organization with products in the UK, AU and US markets.

Cultivating a Diverse Design Skill Set

Over a ten year period I was able to lead many different design tasks while experiencing the challenges that came with it. These tasks ranged from App design, Responsive design, HTML emails, branding and print media .

Tasks were often repeated for new iterations or completely new projects which meant I was able to learn from past challenges and hone my approaches for each skill set.

As I balanced multiple tasks it was also necessary for me to optimize my workflow by refining my process to anticipate potential risks and factor in scalability requirements.

Product Ownership

As I was leading design and front end teams I took a lot of pride in maintaining systems and considering the future direction and vision of the products I worked on.

A business principle was to respond quickly and release often. Anticipating and working ahead of time were skills I needed to develop in regards to this, allowing design iterations to maintain consistency and flexibility as they progressed through changes.

Multi platform product and scalable design system

The products I worked on were part of a multi product family including more than 20 brands and upwards of 500 websites and apps.

Various platforms were also present including marketing and customer facing websites, apps, social media, admin tools for internal users and external clients as well as email and printed media.

To simplify management of this; design systems, UI components, screen templates, SaSS CSS systems were just a few of the items I needed to develop and maintain.

Experience with tech stacks and risk management

In addition to leading the design team I also worked as a front end developer at various times during my career.

I learnt various skills including CSS, JS, HTML, MySQL, PHP, SEO to name just a few. While these skills age over time and get replaced by newer tech the challenges I experienced and the repeated nature of these challenges has meant I have developed a great understanding and intuition for risk management. The insights I learnt are still applicable to this day and allow me to facility understanding when working alongside engineers, product managers and fellow designers.

Take aways from this period

I was able to work dynamically as part of a team and be at the fore of a culture of continuous improvement. At times we cut corners on QA and measuring data affectively. However this period of my career showed me what hard work can produce when a team gets to work.

While I appreciate the processes and decision making steps I have learnt later in my career, I still and hopeful will continue trying to push the needle and see if we can do just a little bit more.
